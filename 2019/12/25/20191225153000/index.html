How to make a Quadruped with Raspberry Pi

Posted on 2019-12-25 Edited on 2019-12-26 In HowTo Views: Valine:

Motivation

I have already forgotten, when and exactly why I started this project to design and build a quadruped myself.

I think I was searching for some ideas to try raspberry out, and then I saw some similar project. How about build one myself? It’s just cool.

Sometime, people ask me about its usage, and to be honest, I couldn’t give an answer.

To explain it, I think it is some kind of the spirit of a maker. Sometimes, you just want to make something not for any purpose, especially not to earn money with it.

You have fun in it and you find it’s cool. That’s enough already.

Parts Design and 3D Printing

First, you need to design the parts in a 3D-CAD software. I used Catia V5 for that. After export the 3d models in a SD card, you can print them out with a 3D printer.



I used ANYCUBIC I3 MEGA to print out all the parts. It is a very cheap model, which costs only about 200€. But it works quite stable and I think its worth the price.

Although, there are some disadvantages of this printer, which caused many problems.

First of all, the precision is poor. In my model, there are a lot of connections, which need a high precision. But the holes printet out are mostly 1-2 mm smaller in the diameter so that the screws wont pass through.
I had to use a small drill to make all the holes bigger.

Secondly, the print speed is very slow. That must be a general problem. But I had to wait hours for each part.

Thirdly, the feeding of the filament sometimes doesnt work well. So you cannot leave the machine alone and you have to alway pay attention on it. Sometimes, the filament just wind up together on the roll, so that the get broken.

If you don’t use it for a while, the nozzle might get clogged. I had to change the nozzle once during my work.

Anyway, finally I managed to print out all the parts and put them together.

Quadruped Control and basic gait

The control system is very easy. I used a raspberry 3B to control the PCA9685, to which 12 servo motors are connected. The PCA9685 can give out the PWM signal so that the rotation of the servo motors can be controlled.

Over this, I used flask to realise a basic web application, so that the raspberry pi monitors a port. And if you click on the direction buttons on the web page. The command will be sent to server with “POST”. In this way, you can remote control the motion of your robot.

The most important part of the control is the so called robotic gait, the movement of the robot. The strategy I used can be explained with the following picture. The red arrows means you have to lift the leg up, move it to the certain angle, and then put it down. The green arrow means, you move the leg directly to certain angle.

With this strategy, you have to ensure that your robot always stays in a stable position. That means your gravity center must be in the triangle formed by the three legs which are not lifted.

Result and lessons learned

As a result, my quadruped 1.0 can finally walk on the floor.

However, there are several problems:

  • The robot is too heavy. At first, I wanted to choose a servo motor with a higher power. So I chose MG995 against SG90. The bigger MG995 has a torque of 9.40 kg-cm at 4.8V and the smaller SG90 has only 1.80 kg-cm. But the MG995 weight 55g and the SG90 weight only 9g.
    Furthermore, the whole dimension and weight of the robot will be too high. Therefore, I would choose the smaller lightweight servo motor for this use.

  • The dimension and gravity center must be redesigned. In this model, The main part is too big and the legs are too short, so that every step, it can only move forward a very short distance. If it moves longer, the gravity center will move out and the robot will fall.
